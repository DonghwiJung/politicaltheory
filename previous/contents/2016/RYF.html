Recollecting Yue Fei

발표자: Leo K. Shin (Professor of History and Asian Studies, University of British Columbia)

일시: 2016년 4월 27일(수) 16시 ~ 17시 45분

장소: 서울대학교 인문대 신양관 국제회의실


초록:

As it is well known, much of what we know about Yue Fei 岳飛 (1103–1142), the quintessential Chinese loyalist, comes from two collections of documents—the Jintuo Compendium of the Prince of E (Eguo Jintuo cui bian 鄂國金佗稡編) and the Supplements to the Jintuo Compendium (Eguo Jintuo xu bian 鄂國金佗續編)—that were compiled long after the general’s death. While scholars have long made use of these two compilations by Yue Ke 岳珂 (1183–1242?), a grandson of Yue Fei, as depositories of biographical information about the Song-dynasty (960–1279) general, relatively little attention has been given to the fact these texts have also been vehicles for the construction and transmission of the images of Yue Fei. As part of a book-length study on the uses of the Song loyalist in later imperial and modern China (and, to a lesser extent, Chosŏn Korea and Tokugawa Japan), this chapter/paper traces the transmission of the Jintuo Compendia and explores how they have over time intersected with other genres of materials related to Yue Fei. In doing so, I hope to show that, rather than think of lives and texts as occupying distinct spheres, one should consider biographical writings—in this case, the compilations by Yue Ke—an integral part of the making of their subjects.


추천 자료:

  • Shin, Leo, "History of the Margins." In The Making of the Chinese State: Ethnicity and Expansion on the Ming Borderlands, 1-19. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2006.
