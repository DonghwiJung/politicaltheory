To what extent was Ming China a “Han Chinese” dynasty?

발표자: Leo K. Shin (Professor of University of British Columbia)

일시: 2016년 4월 28일(목) 18시 30분 ~ 21시

장소: 서울대학교 사회과학대학(16동) 349호 국제회의실


초록:

Although scholars have often maintained that the Ming (1368–1644) was a “Han Chinese” dynasty, what that means—and how the category of “Han” should be understood in the Ming-dynasty context—has not been adequately examined. The textual sources that might shed light on the issue have of course long existed, but recent efforts to render them in digital forms have opened up new research opportunities as well as posted new challenges. This paper is a preliminary attempt to take advantage of selected newly-developed “text-mining” techniques to answer some long-standing questions. In particular, it focuses on the Veritable Records of the Ming (Ming shi lu 明實錄) and seeks to understand how the idea of “Han” was manifested in official discourses. To be sure, there is nothing that can replace sensitive and careful reading of the sources, but judicious uses of digital tools, it appears, do allow us to visualize broader patterns.


추천 자료:

  • Elliott, Mark, "Hushuo: The Northern Other and the Naming of the Han Chinese." InCritical Han Studies: The History, Representation, and Identity of China's Majority, 173-90. Berkeley: University of California Press, 2012.
