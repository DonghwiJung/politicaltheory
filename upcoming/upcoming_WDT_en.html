Why Does the State Have the Right to Control Immigration?

Why, if at all, does the state have the right to control immigration? Contemporary political theorists and philosophers writing about immigration have advanced four types of answers based on 1) the value of distinctive cultures, 2) the right to private property, 3) the value of freedom of association, and 4) the right to be free from unwanted obligations. I discuss the limits of these justifications, propose some desiderata that a compelling theory of the stateâ€™s right to control immigration must meet, and advance an alternative justification based on the idea of collective self-determination.
